Nikola Tesla

Innovative, brilliant, visionary, eccentric, enigmatic.

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla is born on July 10 in Smiljan, Croatia.
  • 1875 - Tesla enrolls at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, Austria.
  • 1882 - Tesla moves to Paris, France to work for the Continental Edison Company.
  • 1884 - Tesla moves to the United States and begins working for Thomas Edison's company.
  • 1887 - Tesla develops an AC motor and founds the Tesla Electric Company.
  • 1891 - Tesla invents the Tesla coil, a transformer for producing high-voltage, low-current electricity.
  • 1893 - Tesla's AC system is chosen for the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago, marking the beginning of the AC vs. DC power struggle.
  • 1895 - Tesla begins research on wireless communication and develops the Tesla oscillator.
  • 1899 - Tesla moves to Colorado Springs, Colorado to conduct high-voltage, high-frequency experiments.
  • 1900 - Tesla returns to New York and begins construction on Wardenclyffe Tower, intended to be a wireless communication and power transmission system.
  • 1901 - Tesla secures funding from J.P. Morgan to continue work on Wardenclyffe Tower.
  • 1903 - Tesla's financial problems force him to abandon the Wardenclyffe Tower project.
  • 1915 - Tesla files for several patents related to his inventions, including a remote control boat and a vertical takeoff aircraft.
  • 1943 - Nikola Tesla dies on January 7 in New York City at the age of 86.
  • 1960 - The International System of Units officially adopts the term "tesla" to measure magnetic flux density, in honor of Nikola Tesla's contributions to electromagnetism.
